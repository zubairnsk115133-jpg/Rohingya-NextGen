About

    Hanifi is a written form of the Rohingya dialect developed in the 1980s by Rohingya scholar, Mohammed Hanif. In 2024, Children on the Edge received permission to pilot using the Hanifi script to facilitate education, marking the first time Rohingya students were permitted to use their mother tongue in the classroom

R

A mother tongue is the first language a person learns from birth, usually spoken by their family and community. It is not only a means of communication but also a foundation of identity, culture, and heritage. Through the mother tongue, children absorb traditions, values, and the unique worldview of their people. The mother tongue plays a vital role in shaping thought, learning, and emotional expression. It connects generations, preserves history, and strengthens the sense of belonging. Respecting and nurturing one’s mother tongue ensures the survival of cultural diversity and enriches both personal and collective identity.
